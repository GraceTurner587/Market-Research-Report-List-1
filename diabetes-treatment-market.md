<p><p>The diabetes treatment market comprises three main types: insulin, oral hypoglycemic drugs, and non-insulin injectable drugs. Insulin is essential for type 1 diabetes and some type 2 cases, helping to regulate blood sugar levels. Oral hypoglycemic drugs are commonly used for type 2 diabetes, promoting insulin secretion or improving insulin sensitivity. Non-insulin injectable drugs, like GLP-1 receptor agonists, aid in blood sugar management by enhancing glucose-dependent insulin secretion and reducing appetite, offering additional options for patients seeking effective treatment.</p></p>
