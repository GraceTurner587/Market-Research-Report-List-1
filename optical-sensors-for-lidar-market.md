<p><p>Optical sensors for LiDAR can be categorized into non-scanning and scanning LiDAR systems. Non-scanning LiDAR employs a fixed array of sensors, capturing data in a broad area simultaneously, enhancing speed and environmental adaptability. In contrast, scanning LiDAR uses a moving laser beam to systematically collect data across a designated area, offering high-resolution, detailed measurements suited for mapping and terrain analysis. Both types serve various applications, from autonomous vehicles to environmental monitoring, driven by the need for precise spatial information.</p></p>
