<p><p>The Cancer Biomarkers market consists of two primary types: protein biomarkers and genetic biomarkers. Protein biomarkers are molecules produced by cancer cells or in response to cancer, aiding in diagnosis, prognosis, and monitoring treatment response. Genetic biomarkers, on the other hand, are alterations in DNA or RNA that indicate susceptibility to certain cancers or predict treatment effectiveness. Both types serve critical roles in personalized medicine, enhancing the precision of cancer detection and targeted therapies, thus improving patient outcomes.</p></p>
