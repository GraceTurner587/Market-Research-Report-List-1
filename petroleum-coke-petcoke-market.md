<p><p>Petroleum coke (petcoke) is a carbon-rich solid byproduct from oil refining, categorized into different types based on its physical and chemical properties. Needle coke is characterized by its high-density and graphitic structure, making it suitable for electrodes in electric arc furnaces. Shot coke features a porous structure, often used in the aluminum industry. Sponge coke has a lower density and is primarily used as a fuel or in anodes. Honeycomb coke, noted for its unique structure, is utilized in specific niche applications.</p></p>
