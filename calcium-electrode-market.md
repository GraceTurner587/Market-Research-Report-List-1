<p><p>The calcium electrode market includes two primary types: liquid membrane and PVC membrane electrodes. Liquid membrane electrodes use a liquid phase that selectively interacts with calcium ions, offering high sensitivity and fast response times, making them ideal for laboratory settings. In contrast, PVC membrane electrodes are made from solid PVC materials that facilitate ion exchange. They are durable and cost-effective, often utilized in field applications and industrial processes. Both types serve critical roles in measuring calcium concentrations across various applications.</p></p>
